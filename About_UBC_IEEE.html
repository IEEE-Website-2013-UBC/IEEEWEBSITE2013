Teams

E.M.B. Soc

Engineering in Medicine and Biology Society (EMBS)

EMBS is a team that reaches out to students who are interested in both engineering and medicine by providing opportunities for them to gain experience in the biomedical industry through medical company tours and seminars.
We want students to gain stronger knowledge of what the biomedical engineering industry is like and to expose to them the responsibilities that biomedical engineers have to take when developing medical products.
To this end, we have toured medical companies such as Verathon Medical, McKesson and Kardium.

P.E. Soc

Power and Energy Society

The IEEE UBC Student Branch PES team works to advance knowledge by providing a communication stream between students and industry. This year the team provided this communication channel through social events and site visits. The team hosted a Student & Industry Networking Event first term and several site visits second term. There were four site visits second term including Powertech Labs and UBC North Substation.
Receiving great feedback from students and industry we have generated demand for future PES events. It is safe to say that we have had a successful year in starting to bridge the gap between education and industry and look forward to another great year.

W. in E. Soc

Women in Engineering Society

The Woman in Engineering IEEE is in charge of making the engineering community at UBC more inclusive and to promote gender equality in the electrical and computer engineering profession. This is done through workshops, professional events and coffee house discussions. Our goal is to create a supportive network for all electrical and computer engineering females students and to improve our community as a whole. Woman in Engineering IEEE is a group that welcomes and encourages the participation of both male and female students.

F.T. Team

Field Trip Team

The field trip team has been hard at work planning our sixth annual international field trip to France and Germany. They have arranged visits to top technology companies and universities such as BMW, Siemens, Mozilla, and Institut Superieur d'Electronique de Paris. The team of six ECE undergraduate students is responsible for all aspects of the twelve day trip. This includes choosing a destination, setting up tours, gathering funding, budgeting, flights, accommodation, transportation, advertising, and registration.

T.E. Team

Think Engineering Team

Think Engineering (TE) is an annual networking event organized and hosted by the IEEE Student Branches at UBC, SFU and BCIT in conjunction with the UBC Electrical and Computer Engineering Student Society. TE2014 team is the largest team with 22 members aiming for one single goal: to deliver the best and improved Think Engineering up to date. Think Engineering aims to connect and engage students in Greater Vancouver with industry representatives from various field of engineering.
The seventh annual Think Engineering event was held at Pan Pacific Vancouver, with over 270 participants. We managed to secure 9 direct sponsors, more than what we achieved in the past years, with addition of 5 more participating companies. The event went very well, and feedback received from both students and industry representatives clearly indicate our success this year and their intention of going/sponsoring again in the years to come.
